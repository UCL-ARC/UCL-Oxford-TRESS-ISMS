# Research Data Information Security Management System
# Overview

## Introduction

The UCL Research Data Information Security Management System (ISMS) exists to secure the data used for research by the university and its collaborators. UCL is a large research-intensive institution with a significant amount of research involving sensitive data within multiple data domains such as health, social sciences and education.

UCL delivers services to support research on sensitive data at UCLincluding the provision of Trusted Research Environments. UCL TREs provide transformative benefits to research by unlocking computing at scale without compromising security.

## Scope

![Scope Image](/docs/Controlled_Documents/Images/ISMSDiagrams-Scope.drawio.png)

The Organisation is made up of:

- Trusted research environments in the ISD and ARC.  Trusted Research Environments are any tier 3 or 4 environment as defined in the [Data Classification and Tiering Policy](./RISM06-Data_Classification_and_Environment_Tiering_Policy.md).
- Researchers who use Trusted Research Environments managed by ARC and ISD. These teams are comprised of members of staff, including honorary staff, and students along with external research collaborators. 
- Members of the teams managing Trusted Research Environments from ISD and ARC.
- Subsets of the research data team (Data Stewards) within the Centre for Advanced Research Computing team providing information governance advisory services to UCL researchers.
- Subsets of UCL Human Resources and Estates divisions providing supporting services associated with Trusted Research Environments.

## Environments, Studies and Projects

| Element | Definition |
|---------|------------|
|Environment | The technical environment where in scope information is stored and processed|
|Study | An information governance construct which defines who owns research data and how those data can be used. |
| Project | A technical boundary within the Environment(s) which defines and controls which users have access to data. |
| | |


![Environment, Study and Project Diagram](/docs/Controlled_Documents/Images/ISMSDiagrams-ISMS%20highest%20level%20view.drawio.png)

Each Environment is assigned a sensitivity tier which defines the type of data that can be stored and processed within the environment boundary. Tier 3 and 4 Environments are within the Scope of the ISO Certification.
