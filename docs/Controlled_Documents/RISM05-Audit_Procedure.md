## 1. Document Overview

Internal audits are conducted at planned intervals to verify whether the Information Security Management System (ISMS) conforms to relevant standards (e.g., ISO 27001) and is effectively implemented and maintained.

## 2. Standards and Methods

Internal audits shall compare organisational processes and practices against:

- Applicable clauses of ISO/IEC 27001  
- Controls recorded in the Statement of Applicability  
- Additional necessary organisational controls  

All sections of the standard shall be audited over a full certification period, with priority given to areas impacted by severe or intolerable risks, incidents, non-conformities, or organisational changes.  

Audits shall be planned and participants notified unless circumstances require otherwise. Audit activities may include interviews, document reviews, and process observations. Audit scope and criteria shall be documented in the [Audit Schedule](<AUDIT_SCHEDULE_LINK>).


## 3. Frequency

Audit frequency shall be regularly reviewed to ensure all standard clauses and controls are covered over the certification period. Additional audits may be scheduled as needed due to risk, incident, or change management considerations.


## 4. Responsibilities

Internal auditors shall be selected to ensure objectivity and impartiality. Auditors must not audit areas in which they are directly involved. Audits shall be conducted by suitably qualified staff or contractors. Role definitions and responsibilities are detailed in the <ORG_NAME> roles and responsibilities framework (<ROLES_DOC_LINK>).


## 5. Protection of Information During Audit

- Auditors shall access only the documents and information required for the audit.  
- Information shall be handled according to its classification and applicable policies.  
- Audit activities shall be planned to minimise disruption to normal operations. Participants may be available on-call or in observation mode where direct involvement is unnecessary.


## 6. Audit Findings and Reporting

- Audit findings shall be documented by the auditor and communicated to the <GOVERNANCE_BODY> (e.g., ISMS Lead).  
- Findings shall be reviewed by <OPERATIONAL_MANAGEMENT_BODY>.  
- Actions arising from findings shall be assigned a reference number, owner, due date, and status, and tracked to closure.  
- Non-conformities shall be addressed with corrective actions and may be escalated to <RISK_APPROVAL_BODY>.


## 7. Root Cause Analysis

- All non-conformities shall be assigned a root cause.  
- Root causes shall be periodically reviewed by <OPERATIONAL_MANAGEMENT_BODY>.  
- Remediation actions shall be assigned as appropriate to prevent recurrence.
