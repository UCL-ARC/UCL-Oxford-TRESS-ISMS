# University Research Data ISMS Overview

*White‑labelled, ISO/IEC 27001:2022 aligned*

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Framework Overview

This framework defines a **complete Research Data Information Security Management System (ISMS)** for `<University Name>`. It enables the secure management of research data across its lifecycle while supporting collaboration, compliance, and assurance.

The framework: 
- Is fully aligned with **ISO/IEC 27001:2022**
- Is designed for **university research data environments**
- Supports **Trusted Research Environments (TREs)**
- Is reusable, auditable, and certification‑ready

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Purpose and Objectives

### 2.1 Purpose

To establish a structured, risk‑based approach for protecting research data and research systems at `<University Name>`, ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

### 2.2 Objectives

-   Protect sensitive, confidential, and regulated research data
-   Enable secure research collaboration and data sharing
-   Meet legal, ethical, contractual, and funder requirements
-   Provide assurance to regulators, partners, and funders
-   Support continual improvement of research data security

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Scope of the Research Data ISMS

### 3.1 In‑Scope

-   Research data processed under the authority of `<University Name>`
-   Trusted Research Environments and research platforms
-   Supporting infrastructure, networks, and applications
-   Staff, students, visiting researchers, contractors, and collaborators
-   Third‑party services used for research processing

### 3.2 Out‑of‑Scope

Explicit exclusions must be documented, justified, approved through ISMS governance, and reviewed regularly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Governance and Organisational Structure

### 4.1 Governance Model

A three‑tier governance structure applies:

1.  **Senior Information Risk Owner (SIRO)** - Executive accountability
2.  **Information Risk Governance Committee (IRGC)** - Strategic oversight
3.  **Operational Management Group (OMG)** - Day‑to‑day ISMS operation

### 4.2 Accountability

Governance ensures:

-  Clear decision-making authority
- Transparent risk ownership
- Alignment with academic and institutional governance
------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

Defined roles include:

-  **ISMS Manager** – coordination, maintenance, reporting
-  **Information Asset Owners** – ownership of research datasets
-  **System / TRE Owners** – secure operation of platforms
-  **Researchers and Data Users** – compliant use of data
-  **IT and Security Teams** – technical control implementation

Each role has defined authority, responsibilities, competence requirements, and training obligations.

## 7. Risk Management

### 7.1 Risk‑Based Approach

The ISMS applies a structured methodology to: 
-  Identify assets
-  Assess threats and vulnerabilities
-  Evaluate likelihood and impact
-  Prioritise risks

### 7.2 Risk Treatment

Risks are treated through: 
-  Control implementation
-  Risk acceptance
-  Risk transfer
-  Risk avoidance
  
All decisions are recorded in the **Risk Register** and **Statement of Applicability (SoA)**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Control Implementation

### 8.1 Control Domains

Controls span: 
- Organisational
- People
- Physical
- Technological

### 8.2 Research‑Specific Controls

-   Trusted Research Environment safeguards
-   Strong identity and access management
-   Secure storage and encryption
-   Logging, monitoring, and auditing
-   Backup, recovery, and resilience measures
