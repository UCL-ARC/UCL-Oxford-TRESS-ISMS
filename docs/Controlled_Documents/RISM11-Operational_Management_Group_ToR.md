## 1. Document Overview

This document defines the **<ISMS_NAME> Operational Management Group (OMG)**, including its purpose, responsibilities, composition, membership, communication approach, and administrative arrangements.

## 2.	Purpose

The Research Data ISMS Operational Management Group monitors the development, implementation, and ongoing management of the organisation’s Information Security Management System (ISMS). The Group’s purpose is to ensure the day-to-day operation of risk management, compliance and continual improvement of information security within the scope of the ISMS, and to provide assurance to IRGC in this respect.

## 3.	Responsibilities of the OMG

The OMG is part of the [Information Governance Framework](RISM02-Roles_and_Responsibilities.md#3-the-information-governance-framework). Subject to any general or particular direction that may from time to time be given by the IRGC, the OMG is charged by IRGC to:

- Review risks and ensure that the risk assessment, risk treatment plan, and statement of applicability remain current.
- Determine, review and monitor operational metrics and targets, monitoring trends.
- Ensure the consequences of planned non-standard changes are fully considered.
- Participate in, receive reports on, and respond to audits.
- Receive and respond to incidents reports and near misses, deciding what actions arise once an incident is closed
- Ensure that OMG activities are scheduled and tracked
- Report to the Information Risk Governance Committee (IRGC) on the performance of the ISMS
- Escalate risks to IRGC where the risk is either ‘severe’ or ‘intolerable’
- Manage control implementations to mitigate risks
- Approve and review policies relating to information security
- Ensure that personnel of the Organisation and relevant interested parties receive appropriate information security awareness, education and training and regular updates on the Organisation's information security policy, topic-specific policies and procedures, as relevant for their job function
- Discussions designed to achieve consensus across the Organisation on policies or how to implement controls



## 2. Purpose

The purpose of the OMG is to monitor and support the development, implementation, operation, and continual improvement of the organisation’s Information Security Management System.

The OMG ensures effective day-to-day management of:

- Risk management activities  
- Information security compliance  
- Operational security performance  
- Incident management oversight  
- Continuous improvement initiatives  

within the defined <ISMS_SCOPE>.



## 3. Responsibilities of the OMG

The OMG forms part of the <INFORMATION_GOVERNANCE_FRAMEWORK_NAME> and has the following responsibilities:

- Review information security risks and ensure the Risk Register, Risk Assessment, Risk Treatment Plan, and Statement of Applicability remain current.
- Monitor ISMS operational performance metrics and trends.
- Review the consequences of planned changes and assess their information security implications.
- Participate in internal and external audits and review audit reports and findings.
- Receive, review, and respond to information security incidents and post-incident reports.
- Ensure OMG activities and actions are recorded, tracked, and closed.
- Report on ISMS performance to the <GOVERNING_COMMITTEE_NAME>.
- Escalate risks to the <GOVERNING_COMMITTEE_NAME> where risk ratings exceed defined tolerance thresholds.
- Oversee the implementation of information security controls.
- Review and approve information security policies, procedures, and standards.
- Support the promotion of information security awareness, education, and training across the organisation.
- Facilitate cross-functional discussions to achieve organisational alignment on ISMS controls and implementation.



## 4. Composition

### 4.1 Membership

The OMG is made up of representatives from relevant stakeholder groups, including:

- <GOVERNING_COMMITTEE_NAME> representatives  
- Technical Environment Owners / Administrators  
- Information Governance Representatives  
- ISMS Operations Team  
- Information Security Team  
- Information Asset Owners  
- Business or Research Representatives  
- Other nominated stakeholders  

Membership roles and representation should reflect the scope and operating model of the <ISMS_NAME>.



### 4.2 Chair

- The OMG shall be chaired by a nominated member selected by group consensus or formal appointment.
- The chair’s responsibilities include meeting facilitation, decision management, and reporting to the <GOVERNING_COMMITTEE_NAME>.



### 4.3 Quorum

The OMG shall be considered quorate when the following are present:

- The chair (or delegated representative)  
- At least one Technical Environment Owner / Administrator  
- At least one additional voting member  



## 5. Meeting Frequency and Structure

The OMG should meet at least **<MEETING_FREQUENCY>**to ensure alignment with the reporting timelines for the <GOVERNING_COMMITTEE_NAME>.

Additional meetings may be convened due to:

- Security incidents  
- Major changes  
- Urgent risks  
- Significant audit findings  

### Standard Meeting Agenda

The standard OMG agenda should include:

- Review of previous meeting minutes and actions  
- Review of ISMS operational metrics and risk trends  
- Review of incidents, audit findings, and lessons learned  
- Review of changes, internal or external developments affecting the ISMS  
- Review of compliance issues and control effectiveness  
- Any Other Business (AOB)  


## 6. Decision-Making Approach

- The OMG shall aim to operate on a **consensus basis**.  
- Where consensus cannot be reached, the Chair shall make the final decision.  
- Where decisions impact areas not represented at a meeting, proposals shall be shared with all OMG members electronically for review. Members shall be given at least <DECISION_RESPONSE_TIME> to object.  
- Any decisions affecting risk exposure beyond agreed appetite or requiring significant resources shall be escalated to the <GOVERNING_COMMITTEE_NAME>.
