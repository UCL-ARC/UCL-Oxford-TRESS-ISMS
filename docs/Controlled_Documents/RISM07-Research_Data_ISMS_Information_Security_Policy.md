## 1. Document Overview

Information is a critical organisational asset, both in support of world-leading research and in the effective management of services and resources.

It is therefore essential that information is managed securely and appropriately, and that policies, procedures, accountability mechanisms and governance structures are in place to provide a robust framework for information security management.

This policy aligns with the organisation’s:
- <ORG_DATA_PROTECTION_POLICY_NAME>
- <ORG_INFORMATION_SECURITY_POLICY_NAME>

Research projects may receive information from third parties such as healthcare organisations, government departments, or commercial partners, which may impose contractual, regulatory, or legal requirements. As a result, the organisation is subject to enhanced information governance responsibilities and must safeguard sensitive and highly confidential information.

### 1.1 ISO 27001:2022 Alignment

This document addresses the following ISO/IEC 27001:2022 requirements:

- Clause 5.2 – Policy
- Clause 6.2 – Information Security Objectives
- Clause 9.1 – Monitoring, Measurement, Analysis and Evaluation

## 4. Information Security Objectives

The Information Security Objectives are owned by the <SIRO_TITLE>.

The primary objective of the organisation is to enable secure research on sensitive data while managing information risks within an acceptable risk appetite and tolerance.

Progress towards this objective shall be monitored using the following metrics:

| Measure | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Use of approved Trusted Research Environments | 100% of registered studies use approved trusted environments |
| Compliance with ISO/IEC 27001 Standard | All relevant controls audited during each certification period |
| Organisational risk level | All risks within defined risk appetite |
| Non-conformity management | 100% of non-conformities acted upon within agreed timeframes |
| Incident management response | 100% of severe incidents addressed according to policy |
| Approved researcher training compliance | ≥ 95% of in-scope users hold current training certification |



### 4.1 Monitoring and Evaluation

- Policy implementation and compliance shall be monitored by the <INFORMATION_SECURITY_GOVERNANCE_BODY>.
- Performance should be evaluated using data from:
  - Training records  
  - Internal and external audit results  
  - Incident reports  
  - Risk assessments  
- Compliance shall be reviewed during incident investigations.


### 4.2 Continual Improvement

Any opportunities for improvement identified through audits, incidents, or near-miss events should be documented, tracked and acted upon according to the organisation’s continuous improvement process.
