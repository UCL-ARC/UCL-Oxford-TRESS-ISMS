## 1. Overview

This guide provides a comprehensive, step-by-step approach for implementing an **Information Security Management System (ISMS)** at `<University Name>`, based on a **research-data-centric operating model**.

It is designed to:
- Support secure research and innovation
- Enable Trusted Research Environments (TREs)
- Align with ISO/IEC 27001:2022
- Meet funder, legal, and regulatory requirements

---

## 2. Implementation Principles

- Risk-based decision-making
- Proportional security controls
- Research enablement first
- Clear accountability
- Continuous improvement (Plan–Do–Check–Act)

---

## 3. Implementation Phases

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|------|----------|------|
| Phase 1 | Months 1–3 | Governance, scope, policy foundation |
| Phase 2 | Months 4–9 | Technical and procedural implementation |
| Phase 3 | Months 10–12 | Service launch and operationalisation |
| Phase 4 | Ongoing | Optimisation and maturity |

---

## 4. Phase 1 – Foundation and Planning

### 4.1 Executive Commitment

- Appoint a **Senior Information Risk Owner (SIRO)**
- Approve ISMS scope and objectives
- Commit resources and funding
- Endorse information security as a research enabler

### 4.2 Stakeholder Identification

Key stakeholders typically include:
- Senior leadership
- IT Services
- Research Services
- Legal and Compliance
- Data Protection Officer
- Information Security

---

## 5. Scope Definition (RISM01)

### Included
- Research data classified as Confidential or Restricted
- Research platforms and Trusted Research Environments
- Supporting infrastructure and services
- Staff, students, contractors, and collaborators

### Excluded
- General corporate IT systems (unless used for research)
- Student administration systems
- Finance and HR systems

All exclusions must be documented and approved.

---

## 6. Governance Structure (RISM02 & RISM11)

### 6.1 Information Risk Governance Committee (IRGC)

Responsibilities:
- Strategic oversight
- Policy approval
- Risk escalation
- Assurance reporting

### 6.2 Operational Management Group (OMG)

Responsibilities:
- Day-to-day ISMS operation
- Risk management
- Incident coordination
- Control monitoring

---

## 7. Risk Management (RISM04)

### 7.1 Risk Assessment

- Identify research information assets
- Assess threats and vulnerabilities
- Evaluate likelihood and impact
- Record risks in a Risk Register

### 7.2 Risk Treatment

Risk treatment options:
- Mitigate
- Accept
- Transfer
- Avoid

All decisions must be reflected in the **Statement of Applicability (SoA)**.

---

## 8. Control Implementation

Controls cover:
- Organisational controls
- People controls
- Physical controls
- Technological controls

Including:
- Identity and access management
- Secure storage and encryption
- Network security
- Logging and monitoring
- Backup and disaster recovery

---

## 9. Data Classification and Handling (RISM06)

Research data must be classified based on:
- Sensitivity
- Legal and ethical requirements
- Contractual obligations

Handling procedures must align with classification.

---

## 10. Training and Awareness

- Mandatory security awareness training
- Role-based training for high-risk roles
- Approved Researcher onboarding
- Ongoing competence assessment

---

## 11. Incident Management

- Clear reporting and escalation pathways
- Defined response and recovery procedures
- Post-incident review and learning

---

## 12. Monitoring, Audit, and Review

- Defined KPIs and performance metrics
- Regular internal audits
- Annual management reviews
- Continuous improvement actions

---
