## 1. Document Overview

The purpose of this policy is to define the organisational requirements for managing change within the scope of the <ISMS_NAME>.  

All processes used to manage changes within supported environments shall adhere to this policy.  
Changes to any supporting IT services, platforms, applications, or infrastructure shall also comply with this policy.



## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all departments, personnel, contractors and third parties involved in the development, management, maintenance or modification of information systems within the scope of the <ISMS_NAME>.

This includes, but is not limited to, changes affecting:

- Software systems and services
- Documented information (including policies, procedures, standards and guidelines)
- Hardware and network infrastructure
- Security controls and system configurations
- Trusted Research Environments or equivalent secure environments (<SECURE_ENVIRONMENT_NAME>)



## 3. Managing Change

All changes shall be recorded and documented using an approved Change Record.

Change records shall be retained in accordance with organisational record retention requirements and made available to the <GOVERNANCE_BODY> upon request.

Each Change Record shall include at a minimum the following:

### 3.1 Change Record Requirements

**1. Purpose**  
- What is the reason for the change?

**2. Scope**  
- What services, systems, processes, environments or components will be changed?

**3. Impact**  
- How will this change affect users, services or the organisation as a whole?

**4. Risk Assessment**  
- What risks are introduced during and after the change?
- How will these risks be mitigated or controlled?

**5. Schedule**  
- When will the change be implemented?
- What downtime or service disruption is expected?

**6. Test Plan**  
- What testing will be performed before and after the change?
- What are the acceptance criteria?

**7. Communication Plan**  
- How will stakeholders and users be informed of the change and any potential disruption?

**8. Contingency and Rollback Plan**  
- How will services be restored if the change fails?
- What is the rollback procedure?

**9. Authorisation**  
- Who approved the change?
- What governance or escalation process applies?



## 4. Change Types

All changes shall be classified into one of the following categories.  
Each change type shall have a defined approval route and governance process.

### 4.1 Standard Change
A pre-approved, low-risk, repeatable change is performed frequently and follows an established procedure.

**Example:** Deployment of a research project into a secure environment.



### 4.2 Emergency Change
A change required to resolve or mitigate the impact of a live incident, security breach or service failure where standard processes cannot be followed due to urgency.



### 4.3 Normal Change
Any change that is not classified as a Standard or Emergency change, including planned upgrades, feature additions or non-urgent fixes.
