# Overview
This process allows external collaborators (i.e. researchers outside of UCL engaging in collaborative research with UCL researchers) to access Projects they have been invited to collaborate on. This process is owned by the Research Information Governance (IG) Lead.

## 5 Safes Link
**Safe People, Safe Settings**: External collaborators are subject to the same onboarding requirements as internal UCL users, i.e. appropriate training and the signing of an agreement acknowledging their responsibilities. This makes UCL's TRE's robust to cross-organisation Projects by ensuring collaborators can access TRE Projects they have been invited to join without circumventing standard security controls.

# Process
## Purpose
This process ensures external collaborators can be granted access to the TRE Projects they are invited to join without any loss in confidence in a TRE's security controls.

## Scope
All external users invited to use a UCL TRE that is classified as Tier 3 or below are within the scope of this process. 

## Responsibilities
- Information Asset Owner (IAO): must "sponsor" the invitation of the external collaborator into UCL's Identity and Access Management System.

## Prerequisites
- The external collaborator can only be invited to join UCL's Identity and Access Management System as a Guest user by someone with the role of Information Asset Owner or Information Asset Administrator.

## Steps
1. The external collaborator is invited to the ARC Services Portal by an Information Asset Owner (IAO).
2. If the external collaborator already has a Guest user account in UCL's Identity and Access Management System, the user is added to an "Invited Researcher" security group, and sent a link to the ARC Service Portal.
3. If the external collaborator does not have a Guest user accoun, they are invited to join UCL's Identity and Access Management System as a Guest user. Upon accepting this invitation, the external collaborator's Guest account is activated and this account is added to an "Invited Researcher" security group.
4. The external collaborator completes the "Become an Approved Researcher" process and is granted the role of "Approved Researcher".
5. Upon being granted the role of "Approved Researcher", the external collaborator is removed from the "Invited Researcher" security group and added to the "Approved Researcher" security group.
